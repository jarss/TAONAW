Photoshoot

2019-12-08

I haven'tt picked up my camera for more than a week, and I was itching to take photos. It's a feeling I've known since I could remember feelings. A mix of unrest and uncertinty, much like a dog circuling around the house before a walk is necessary. Well, a walk was necessary.

The weather was an inivting misty gray. The sort of day when rain hasn't fully gave up to the fog, threatening in little needles; nothing like the golden hour recommended in tutorials. In fact, it was mid-day with the sun nowhere to be seen.

I aimed for Fort Tryon. Familiar grounds, and in a weather like that, I was hoping for less humans and more animals. Most of all, I wanted the sort of quiet you learn to pray for in NYC. Being out of practice, I was stuck in my head thinking about my latest computer-related issue1.

It was the quiet, now that I think about it. Turning into a side street muffeled the contstant car “woosh” sounds and horns out of my mind. The street lead to a nicer part of the nighborhood, and a building I passed many times before was shrowded in fog to my left.
